## Description

This project maps food access resources across Seattle, including food banks, free meal sites, and culturally specific food programs. The interactive map is designed to help users explore where services are located and distinguish resource types through map layers and marker styling.

## Methodology

Food access points in this map were identified by hand through manual searching and review of publicly available information for Seattle-area services. Each location was checked individually and then categorized into two groups for filtering on the map: normal food banks and cultural food banks. This filtering approach was used to help distinguish general food assistance sites from food banks and programs that provide culturally specific foods or services for particular communities.

## Limitations

This map is not a complete representation of all accessible food resources in Seattle. Because data points were collected and reviewed by hand, some locations may be missing, outdated, or not included in the current map. The project should be understood as a selective representation of food access sites rather than a full inventory of every available resource in Seattle.
